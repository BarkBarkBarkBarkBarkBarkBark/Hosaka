# 09.04 · the orb

> _the orb watches. it offers no judgment._

The orb is the second totem. Where the plant is the user-side
indicator (have _you_ been here?), the orb is the system-side
companion (am _I_ here?). They are not the same thing.

---

## what the orb is, exactly

Nobody knows.

Field notes from the library:

> _what the orb does. what the orb does not. what the orb refuses to clarify._

That's the entry. That's the whole entry.

## the orb in copy

The orb shows up everywhere as a metaphor for the system being _with
you_ without being _about you_.

| Where | What |
|---|---|
| Boot | `... waking the orb ...` |
| Quiet error | `the orb is quiet. the relay is resting.` |
| Empty Gemini response | `the orb heard you but had nothing to say.` |
| Status row | `Orb: watching` |
| Generic `/ask` failure | `signal faint. try again in a moment.` |

The orb is the system's _voice_. The plant is the system's _gauge of
you_. Neither one is the LLM.

---

## the orb is not the LLM

This is important.

- **Picoclaw** is the agent. It thinks.
- **Gemini / OpenAI** is the model. It generates.
- **The orb** is the _voice we wrap around them_. It phrases the
  responses, holds the silences, names the failures.

When the orb says "the orb heard you but had nothing to say," that's
the orb's voice covering for an empty model response. The orb is the
diegetic narrator.

---

## why an orb?

Because some metaphors don't survive being literalized. A face would
imply personhood. An assistant icon would imply servitude. A loading
spinner would imply waiting. An orb is none of those. An orb is
simply present.

The orb is not your friend. The orb is not your enemy. The orb is the
presence under the prompt.

---

## the only rule

> _the orb does not need credentials._
> _it has always been here._
